From popular releases with strong storytelling to social relevance and cultural impact, these films have not only dominated box offices but also changed the way we understand cinema. Rotten Tomatoes brings you the ultimate movie showdown — top-rated films of all time.

Top list

TOP 1

The Godfather

(1972)

"A film that defined modern cinema."

Francis Ford Coppola's mafia epic remains a landmark in filmmaking. With its unforgettable performances by Marlon Brando and Al Pacino, richly layered script, and haunting score, The Godfather continues to impress critics and public alike.

More than just a gangster film, it is a reflection on power, loyalty, and the American dream gone wrong. Its cinematography, use of silence, and family-centered narrative structure influenced generations of filmmakers.

Runtime: 2h 55min Academy Awards: 3 Rating: 9.2/10
TOP 2

Inception

(2010)

"A dream within a dream... within a cinematic masterpiece."

Christopher Nolan's Inception is widely regarded as one of the most innovative and intellectually ambitious films of the 21st century. At its core, it's a heist film—but one that takes place entirely within dreams, creating a multilayered story that challenges audiences while delivering spectacular action.

The film's visual achievements are equally staggering. Scenes unfold in rotating hallways, folding cities, and collapsing dreamscapes—all achieved through a masterful combination of practical effects, CGI, and physics-defying set pieces.

Runtime: 2h 28min Academy Awards: 4 Rating: 8.8/10
TOP 3

Parasite

(2019)

"A tale of class, survival, and societal tension—told with masterful wit and suspense."

Bong Joon-ho's Parasite is a landmark in global cinema, not only for its historic achievement as the first non-English language film to win the Oscar for Best Picture, but also for the way it captivates audiences across cultural and linguistic boundaries.

What begins as a clever con gradually spirals into a cutting meditation on class disparity, and the film observes how poverty can transform but also bind the poor. The script brilliantly weaves humor, heart, and social commentary.

Runtime: 2h 12min Academy Awards: 4 Rating: 8.6/10
